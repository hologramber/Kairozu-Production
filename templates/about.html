初めまして、I'm Kairozu.

Kairozu uses programmed learning to build an understanding of Japanese grammar using English-to-Japanese sentence construction. Grammatical constructs are introduced in small, logical steps, which are organized by chapter around groups of new vocabulary words.

I find Socratic and programmed methods of inquiry to be particularly effective when it comes to retaining information -- my two favorite examples are Sidman's Neuroanatomy: A Programmed Learning Tool and The Little Schemer. Here I've tried to minimize wordy grammar explanations and maximize immediate application of small grammar templates, which are then built upon word by word (or particle by particle).

I am not a teacher, a programmer, or a designer, nor do I speak Japanese natively. Let's be clear: learning a language from someone that is ALSO learning the language is perhaps not the brightest idea A TERRIBLE IDEA. I've probably DEFINITELY made mistakes and it would be unfortunate if you internalized my mistakes as grammar fact. Building things and trying to explain them to someone else helps me learn, which is to say I made this for myself. Use and learn incorrect things at your own risk.

The lessons are my attempt to organize the grammar I've learned into a format I can easily reference. Most of the Japanese sentences are computer-generated using common words and grammar patterns compiled from Japanese-language sources aimed at beginners, namely NHK Easy News, 福娘, 毎日小学生新聞, 毎日１５歳のニュース, 朝日小学生新聞, 朝日中学生新聞, and FNN News. I talk more about my adventures in analyzing Japanese text on the Kairozu blog.

If you wish to contact me, please read this first: Contact Kairozu.

Basic Introduction

A solid grasp on hiragana and katakana is necessary before attempting any of the material on this site! There are many internet resources available for studying the Japanese syllabaries, a few of them are listed here: Japanese Kana.
1Access new grammar via main -- as chapters become available, they will turn red.
Additional tools and reference material can be found in the sandbox.
Review material is presented via spaced-repetition; available reviews are indicated in the navigation bar.
2Within a chapter, the left sidebar is a linear guide to the vocabulary, expression, grammar, and exercise sections.
You can always review previous content from any chapter; completed sections have a green check.
3Each chapter has 80-100 new vocabulary and 15-20 colloquial expressions that will be used throughout the grammar sections; it's important to learn these first.
4Complete the vocabulary and expression quizzes to unlock the first grammar lesson for that chapter.
Enter vocabulary words and phrases in Japanese without using hints to successfully complete these sections.
5Once you've read the grammar notes, start the practice exercises; these are pairs of computer-generated English sentences which you translate to Japanese.
The practice exercises must be successfully completed in full (in a single session) to unlock the grammar quiz.
6Complete the クイズ for each grammar section to unlock additional lessons. At the end of each chapter, a series of Exercises which help summarize everything you've learned thus far.
7Following the successful completion of all practices, quizzes, and exercises within a chapter, the next chapter will be unlocked.
If you've fallen behind on review material, new chapters will remain locked until you've caught up.
8Vocabulary, Expressions, and Sentences follow a spaced-repetition review pattern. Pending reviews can be accessed through the navigation bar, with new material added to the review queue as you encounter it.
