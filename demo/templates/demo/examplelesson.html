The dog is cute.

いぬ  は  かわいい  です。

The sentence form ｢Ｘ は Ｙ です｣ means "X is Y" or "as for X, (it) is Y." The particle は marks X as the topic of the sentence and is akin to saying "as for X..."; or in the sentence above, "as for the dog, (it) is cute."

X is what you are talking about; he, she, the dog, the cat, I, we, Mr. Smith, etc. Y is the information that you are giving about something; occupation, name, description, etc. The copula です at the end of a sentence is equivalent to the English "am," "is," or "are."

The dog is cute. いぬ　は　かわいい　です。
The cat is cute. ねこ　は　かわいい　です。
 
The bag is small. かばん　は　ちいさい　です。
The bag is new. かばん　は　あたらしい　です。
 
The bag is large. かばん　は　おおきい　です。
The dog is large. いぬ　は　おおきい　です。
The book is large. ほん　は　おおきい　です。
The cat is large. ねこ　は　おおきい　です。

The topic is usually omitted if the listener can infer what you're talking about from the context of the conversation. For example, if someone asked "what is that piece of paper?" you might simply say "a letter" instead of "that piece of paper is a letter."

The form ｢Ｙ です｣ is a complete sentence where the topic is implied or understood, but not directly stated. Both here and in future lessons, if something is in parentheses (or if the context is otherwise described) in the English sentence, it is generally omitted in Japanese.

(It) is big. おおきい　です。 Listener understands what "it" is from the context of the conversation.
(It) is cute. かわいい　です。 Listener understands what "it" is from the context of the conversation.
 
(It) is a dog. いぬ　です。 Listener understands what "it" is from the context of the conversation.
(It) is a book. ほん　です。 Listener understands what "it" is from the context of the conversation.
X　は　Y　です｡ X is Y.
As for X, (it) is Y.
Drop ｢X は｣ if topic can be inferred.
The cat is small.
ねこ  は  ちいさい  です。
The dog is cute.
いぬ  は  かわいい  です。
The book is new.
ほん  は  あたらしい  です。
  • The topic denoted by は, usually coming at the beginning of the sentence, establishes what the sentence is about. It must be someone or something that both the speaker and the listener can see or hear, or identify from previous conversation or shared knowledge.
  • です is polite speech; when speaking with family or friends, だ can be used instead.
